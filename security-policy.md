# Information Security Policy
## KlausDREAD Cybersecurity Company

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-19  
**Prepared by:** Rosie Bandile 🌹  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

---

## 1. PURPOSE
This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting KlausDREAD's information assets, including confidential data, intellectual property, systems, and networks. It applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and other third parties who have access to KlausDREAD's information resources.

## 2. SCOPE
This policy applies to:
- All KlausDREAD information systems, networks, devices, and applications
- All data stored, processed, or transmitted by KlausDREAD
- All physical and logical access to KlausDREAD facilities and resources
- All employees, contractors, and third parties accessing KlausDREAD information
- All locations where KlausDREAD conducts business (offices, remote work, client sites)

## 3. POLICY STATEMENT
KlausDREAD is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of its information assets through appropriate security measures, awareness training, and continuous improvement. All personnel are responsible for adhering to this policy and related procedures.

## 4. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 Management Responsibilities
- Ensure compliance with this policy within their areas of responsibility
- Provide adequate resources for information security implementation
- Ensure personnel receive appropriate security awareness training
- Conduct risk assessments for new initiatives and significant changes
- Respond to security incidents in accordance with incident response procedures

### 4.2 Information Security Officer (ISO) Responsibilities
- Develop, maintain, and update this policy and related procedures
- Conduct regular security assessments and audits
- Monitor security threats and vulnerabilities
- Coordinate incident response efforts
- Serve as point of contact for security inquiries
- Report security metrics to management

### 4.3 Employee Responsibilities
- Comply with all security policies and procedures
- Complete required security awareness training
- Report security incidents or suspected vulnerabilities promptly
- Protect confidential information entrusted to them
- Use company resources appropriately and securely

## 5. ASSET MANAGEMENT

### 5.1 Inventory
- Maintain an inventory of all information assets (hardware, software, data)
- Classify assets based on sensitivity and criticality
- Assign asset owners responsible for protection and maintenance
- Review and update inventory regularly

### 5.2 Classification
Information shall be classified as:
- **Public:** Information approved for public distribution
- **Internal:** Information for internal use only (default classification)
- **Confidential:** Sensitive information requiring protection from unauthorized disclosure
- **Restricted:** Highly sensitive information with strict access controls

### 5.3 Handling
- Assets shall be handled according to their classification
- Confidential and Restricted information requires explicit authorization for access
- Physical and electronic media shall be labeled appropriately
- Secure disposal procedures shall be followed for all media

## 6. ACCESS CONTROL

### 6.1 User Access
- Access shall be granted based on least privilege and separation of duties
- User accounts shall be uniquely assigned to individuals
- Access requests shall follow formal approval processes
- Access shall be reviewed periodically (quarterly)
- Access shall be revoked immediately upon termination or role change

### 6.2 Authentication
- Strong authentication mechanisms shall be used (passwords, MFA, certificates)
- Passwords shall meet complexity requirements and be changed regularly
- Multi-factor authentication shall be required for remote access and privileged accounts
- Shared accounts are prohibited except where absolutely necessary and documented

### 6.3 Network Access
- Network segmentation shall be implemented to isolate sensitive systems
- Remote access shall be secured through VPN with MFA
- Wireless networks shall use WPA2/WPA3 encryption with strong passwords
- Guest networks shall be separated from internal networks

## 7. CRYPTOGRAPHY
- Approved encryption algorithms shall be used for data at rest and in transit
- Key management procedures shall be established for generation, storage, distribution, and destruction
- Cryptographic modules shall be validated to recognized standards (FIPS 140-2/3, etc.)
- Encryption keys shall be protected with appropriate access controls

## 8. PHYSICAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL SECURITY
- Secure areas shall be protected by appropriate entry controls
- Visitors shall be escorted and logged in secure areas
- Equipment shall be protected from environmental threats (fire, flood, temperature)
- Power protection (UPS, generators) shall be provided for critical systems
- Clear desk and clear screen policies shall be enforced in sensitive areas

## 9. OPERATIONS SECURITY
- Documented operating procedures shall be maintained for all systems
- Change management procedures shall control modifications to systems and software
- Capacity planning shall be performed to prevent performance degradation
- Systems shall be protected against malware through preventive controls
- Backup procedures shall be established and tested regularly
- Logging and monitoring shall be implemented to detect security events
- Clock synchronization shall be maintained across all systems

## 10. COMMUNICATIONS SECURITY
- Network security controls shall protect information in transit
- Electronic messaging shall be secured appropriately for sensitive information
- Business information systems shall be protected against threats
- Cryptographic controls shall be used to protect confidential communications
- Security requirements shall be identified for network services

## 11. SYSTEM ACQUISITION, DEVELOPMENT, AND MAINTENANCE
- Security requirements shall be included in all information system procurement
- Systems shall be hardened based on vendor recommendations and industry best practices
- Security controls shall be implemented in application development
- Cryptographic controls shall be properly implemented and tested
- Security of system files shall be ensured through access controls and integrity checks
- Outsourced development shall adhere to security requirements
- Test data shall be protected and controlled
- System acceptance testing shall include security testing
- Controls shall be implemented to protect against technical vulnerabilities

## 12. SUPPLIER RELATIONSHIPS
- Security requirements shall be established for third-party service providers
- Suppliers shall be evaluated for their ability to meet security requirements
- Agreements with third parties shall include security clauses
- Supplier access to KlausDREAD information shall be controlled and monitored
- Supply chain security shall be considered for critical components

## 13. INCIDENT MANAGEMENT
- Responsibilities and procedures for incident management shall be established
- Events shall be reported through appropriate channels in a timely manner
- Incidents shall be assessed and classified according to impact and urgency
- Appropriate actions shall be taken to contain, eradicate, and recover from incidents
- Evidence shall be collected and preserved in accordance with legal requirements
- Incidents shall be documented and reviewed to prevent recurrence
- Incident response procedures shall be tested and updated regularly

## 14. INFORMATION SECURITY ASPECTS OF BUSINESS CONTINUITY MANAGEMENT
- Information security continuity shall be included in business continuity planning
- Redundancy and availability requirements shall be considered for information systems
- Backup and restore procedures shall be tested regularly
- Information security shall be considered during disaster recovery exercises

## 15. COMPLIANCE
- Compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements shall be ensured
- Regular reviews shall be conducted to evaluate compliance with this policy
- Systems shall be regularly tested for compliance with security requirements
- Non-compliance shall be addressed through appropriate corrective actions

## 16. POLICY COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT
- Compliance with this policy shall be monitored through regular audits and assessments
- Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Exceptions to this policy shall be formally documented, approved, and reviewed periodically
- This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or as needed due to significant changes

## 17. DEFINITIONS
- **Confidential Information:** Information not generally known to non-employees that has economic value from not being generally known
- **Information Asset:** Any data, device, or other component of the environment that supports information-related activities
- **Least Privilege:** The principle that users should be given the minimum levels of access needed to perform their job functions
- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Authentication method requiring two or more verification factors
- **Security Incident:** Any adverse event that threatens the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information assets
